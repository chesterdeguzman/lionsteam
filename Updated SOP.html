1. Background: The Operational Structure and Workflow Standardization

Under the current operational setup, content production, scheduling, and task management responsibilities are distributed across multiple team members without a fully centralized system of control. While this structure allows for flexibility, it has also resulted in inconsistencies in execution, missed deliverables, and gaps in accountability, particularly in areas such as scheduling accuracy, revision tracking, and adherence to content standards.

The proposed operational framework introduces a more structured and centralized approach, wherein the Operations Manager assumes full ownership of task allocation, scheduling, monitoring, and quality control. This includes the management of all ClickUp tasks, communication workflows, trackers, and scheduling platforms such as Metricool, ensuring a single point of accountability across all processes.

This shift is driven by the need to improve efficiency, maintain consistency in content delivery, and uphold the premium standards required across all Studio 5-managed brands. While the intent is to enhance collaboration and streamline operations, the implementation represents a significant transition from a loosely coordinated workflow to a highly structured, performance-driven system.

6. Operational Workflow

The Operations Manager holds full authority over workflow execution, task prioritization, and operational decisions. All team members are required to adhere strictly to instructions, timelines, and standards set by the Operations Manager.

6.16.1 Task Management
  • All tasks shall be managed through ClickUp. 
  • Each task must include a clear description, deadline, and deliverables.
  • Tasks must show daily progress. 
  • Completed tasks must be reviewed and marked complete immediately.
6.26.2 Content Production
  • Each client requires 12 content outputs per month, consisting of carousels and reels.
  • All content for the succeeding month must be completed on or before the 20th of the current month.
  • Priority must be given to important dates and events.
6.36.3 Content  Standards and Compliance

All content must comply with internal and client-specific guidelines.

  • No use of M-dashes
  • Include call-to-action elements where applicable

Platform-specific rules must be strictly followed:

  • Google Business Profile: No contact details
  • TikTok: JPEG format only
  • X (Twitter): Use thread format for long captions
6.46.4 Scheduling and Publishing
  • All scheduling shall be centralized under the Operations Manager using Metricool.
  • Content must be reviewed for compliance before scheduling.
  • Revisions must be monitored and resolved promptly.
6.56.5 Asset Management
  • All files must be uploaded and organized in the designated Google Drive structure.
  • Proper naming conventions and folder organization must be maintained at all times.
6.66.6 Calendar Alignment
  • All client content calendars must be aligned to a standardized monthly timeline.
  • Adjustments, including additional content, may be implemented to achieve alignment.
6.76.7 Monitoring and Reporting
  • Daily monitoring of all active tasks is required.
  • Any delays, issues, or risks must be escalated immediately.
6.86.8 Revision Management
  • All revisions must be actioned immediately upon receipt.
  • No revisions request should remain unresolved.
  • Revisions must be applied ONLY within the specific batch where the content belongs.
  • Do NOT update thumbnails, captions, or assets outside the assigned batch.

Example:

If revision is under Batch 5, update:

  • Thumbnail → Batch 5 Canva file only
  • Caption → Batch 5 only

Purpose: Prevents version confusion and maintains clean batch organization.

6.8.16.8.1 Revision Management Part 2 (Documentation Requirement)
  • All revisions must include:
  • Exact changes made
  • Clear tracking format
  • Every revision submission must include:
  • Bullet-point summary of updates
  • Must be sent together with revised output

Required Format:

Example:

Brand: Dr. Libby

May 22, 2026

Revision Notes:

  • Great post, zoom into the lips more

Revisions Applied:

  • Zoomed in the lip photo

Reason: Makes QA faster and more accurate, ensure revisions are actually implemented, and improves accountability and tracking.

6.96.9 Communication Protocol
  • All communications, instructions, and feedback must be documented within ClickUp.
  • Team members must maintain clear and professional communication at all times.


Acknowledgement Rule:

  • All messages (including ClickUp) must be acknowledged

Example:

“Got this! Will work on this after finishing urgent tasks.”

6.106.10 Daily 5PM Check Rule
  • At 5:00 PM daily:
  • Review all assigned tasks

  • Provide status update in ClickUp
6.116.11 Communication Standard
  • Always provide:

6.106.10 Meetings and Documentation

13. Studio 5 Content and Feed Standards

The following standards apply to all brands managed under Studio 5 and must be strictly adhered to in all content production and delivery. These represent the minimum acceptable creative and production quality.

13.113.1 Core Pilosophy
  • Content must be intentional, premium, clean, strategic, and credible.
  • Feeds must appear curated, not templated.
  • Content must align with each brand’s positioning.
13.213.2 Mandatory Post Format
  • All posts must be either Reels or Multi-slide Carousels.
  • Single static posts are not allowed unless approved.
13.313.3 Carousel Standards
  • Minimum of 4 slides (ideally 5–8).
  • Must follow structure: Hook → Context → Insight → Expansion → CTA.
  • Each slide must add value (no fillers).
  • Luxury brands require minimal text and strong visuals.
  • 50% Pure images and 50% must have texts
  • 1 carousel per week can have text and 1 carousel per week with just pure images.
13.413.4 Reel Standards
  • Strong hook within first 2 seconds.
  • Fast-paced, intentional editing.
  • Clean cover image and transitions.
  • Avoid messy or excessive effects.
13.513.5 Visual and Design Standards
  • Max 30% of posts may include heavy text overlays.
  • Avoid clutter and dense layouts.
  • Maintain visual balance across feed.
  • Premium brands require cleaner, image-heavy designs.
13.613.6 Caption Standards
  • Strong hook in first line.
  • Use short, structured paragraphs.
  • UK English only.
  • Tone must match brand voice.
13.713.7 Content Planning
  • Include relevant industry and national dates only.
  • Content must be planned quarterly in advance.
13.813.8 Production Timeline
  • By 15th: Drafts for next month completed.
  • 15th–20th: Review period.
  • By 22nd: Revisions completed.
  • 23rd onwards: Scheduling in Metricool.
  • All posts must be ready at least 5 days before posting.
13.913.9 Quality Control Checklist
  • Content must be visually premium and balanced.
  • Text overlays must be minimal and intentional.
  • Must include relevant dates where applicable.
  • Must follow UK grammar standards.
  • Must be client-ready before submission.
13.1013.10 Performance and Accountability
  • Consistency reduces revision cycles.
  • High performance increases autonomy.
  • Performance incentives may be applied based on quality and deadlines.
13.1113.11 Thumbnail and Page Title Standard
  • All Canva files must include a clear page title, especially for reel thumbnails.
  • Thumbnail titles must match the Google Drive folder naming.

Example:

  • “Talking Head 25 Thumbnail”

14. General Team Notes and Internal Workflow Enhancements

14.114.1 Prevents creative burnout and repetitive outputs.
  • Requests must be sent for new assets.
  • If assets are repetitive or insufficient, team must compile a list and escalate.
14.214.2 Repetitive reels are perceived as “lazy” and reduce engagement quality.
  • Text is NOT always required in reels.
  • Layout
  • Music
  • Transitions
  • Use CapCut templates and vary
  • Avoid identical structure: video + text overlay + same transitions.
  • Reels must NOT reuse the same template across brands.
  • Reels Standard (CRITICAL)
14.314.3 Human content increases engagement and brand relatability.
  • Repetition of team photos is allowed.
  • All brands must include human presence in content (faces, team, owner, lifestyle shots).
  • Human Presence Requirement
14.414.4 Improves discoverability and aligns with platform best practices.
  • Location must match the actual business or relevant tagged place.
  • All posts must include correct business location tagging.
  • Location Tagging (ALL BRANDS)
14.5Carousel Ratio Standard
  • Requirement: Maintain a 50-50 ratio for carousel content.
  • Example: Out of 8 carousels, 4 must be purely image-based and 4 may include text.
  • Reason: Ensures visual balance, avoids text-heavy feeds, and maintains premium aesthetic quality.
14.614.6 Canva Link Management
  • All Canva design links must be shared with the entire team at all times.
  • No private or restricted access links are allowed.
  • This ensures visibility, collaboration, and continuity in case of reassignment.

Reason: Prevents workflow delays and ensures any team member can access and continue tasks without dependency issues.

14.7Carousel Canva File Name Requirement
  • All Canva file titles must include the business name, service category, and posting month/year for better organization and search visibility.
  • Format: [Brand Name] – [Month] [Year] (Batch Number)
  • Example: Bullen’s Jewellers Norwich – May 2026 (1) or N-ERGISE Engineering – May 2026 (1).
  • No inconsistent naming formats are allowed.
Reason: Ensures easier tracking, searching, and organization across platforms.
14.8AI Review Standards
  • Before finalizing AI-assisted visuals, carefully review all faces, hands, and human features to ensure they appear natural and undistorted.
  • Check for anatomy errors, warped objects, duplicated elements, distorted faces, unnatural hands, or unrealistic rendering.
  • All exported content must be checked for visual inconsistencies before scheduling or publishing.
14.9Canva Folder Name Requirement
  • All files must follow a standardized naming convention:
  • Format: [Brand] - [Month]
  • Example: Bullens - MAY
  • No inconsistent naming formats are allowed.

Reason: Ensures easy tracking, searching, and organization across all platforms.

14.10Internal Workflow Practice (MANDATORY)
  • Always add the Title Page AFTER completing feed layout arrangement.
  • The Title Page date must correspond to the posting schedule.

Example: If a carousel is labeled June 1, it must reflect the correct posting order in sequence.

Reason: Improves workflow clarity and reduces dependency on checking layouts repeatedly.

14.11Google Drive Organization (Reels and Assets)
  • All files must include clear and identifiable titles.
  • Avoid vague file names such as “final.mp4” or “video1.mp4”.
  • All final videos must be uploaded with:
  • Caption
  • Thumbnail

Folder Structure:

  • Organize files by MONTH using a mother folder system.

Example:

March Folder

  • All reels and assets scheduled for March
  • March Reels

Note: Always use date or clear title-based folders

Reason: Improves tracking, avoids missing uploads, and ensures quick identification of new vs. completed content.

14.12Reel Video Quality Standard
  • All reels and exported video content must be rendered in 1080p resolution.
  • 1080p is the preferred posting standard because it provides reliable upload quality, playback stability, and platform compatibility across social media channels.
  • Avoid exporting reels in unnecessarily higher resolutions unless specifically required by the client, platform, or campaign.
